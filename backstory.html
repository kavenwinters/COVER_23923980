BACKSTORY

CHAPTER 1: ROOTS & TRADITIONS

This essay explores how my perspective was fundamentally shaped by growing up in Toledo, WA, a small agricultural town in the Pacific Northwest. Our traditions were built on hard work, resilience, and a profound respect for the community that supported us. Living among fields that required patience and dedication taught me traditional values that go beyond the typical definitions of outstanding character.

Our town values dedication and honesty over quick success. It taught me that outstanding service isn’t about applause, but about quietly ensuring the job is done right. As I transitioned from a small farming community to a major university campus, those values grounded me. This background forms the foundation of my drive and my understanding of traditional success.

CHAPTER 2: THE JOURNEY TO PURPOSE

My journey to the University of Arizona required navigating challenges that extended beyond mere academics. Arriving on campus initially presented a complex landscape of traditions and new ideas. But it was here that I found my true calling and my dedication to the larger community.

My work in mechanical engineering and wind power research has been driven by that same small-town belief that every action has purpose. I learned that true service often happens behind the scenes, upholding the traditions of scholarship and community while preparing for future challenges. My perspective shifted from merely seeking involvement to actively contributing to the enduring spirit that drives the Wildcat community forward.
